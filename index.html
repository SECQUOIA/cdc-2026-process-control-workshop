01 / Scope

Why this workshop

Machine learning is entering plant operations, decarbonization is changing operating objectives, and scheduling and control are becoming more tightly coupled. The workshop examines the control problems created by these developments.

01

Learning-based control

Model predictive control, safe reinforcement learning, Bayesian optimization, hybrid models, and state estimation—with attention to guarantees and plant deployment.

Methods and guarantees
02

Decarbonized operation

Electricity prices, renewable supply, and carbon accounting make set points, constraints, and objectives time-dependent.

Energy and emissions
03

Integrated decisions

Scheduling, real-time optimization, and feedback control must work together across timescales from milliseconds to days.

Scheduling and control

02 / Discussion questions

Questions for theory and practice

01

What has changed?

Where do learning-based methods improve on established approaches, and where do classical methods remain preferable?

02

What remains unresolved?

Safety, uncertainty, closed-loop data efficiency, interpretable policies, and integration across timescales.

03

What can be deployed?

Methods that account for process constraints, changing objectives, and the practical limits of plant data and computation.
